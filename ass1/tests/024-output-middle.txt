I had thought up to that moment of the adventures before me,not at all of the home that I was leaving; and now, at sight of thisclumsy stranger, who was to stay here in my place beside my mother, Ihad my first attack of tears. I am afraid I led that boy a dog's life,for as he was new to the work, I had a hundred opportunities of settinghim right and putting him down, and I was not slow to profit by them.The night passed, and the next day, after dinner, Redruth and I wereafoot again and on the road. I said good-bye to Mother and thecove where I had lived since I was born, and the dear old AdmiralBenbow--since he was repainted, no longer quite so dear.
